Sign up for the Osterweis Tournament (April 7th)

March 31, 2013 7:24 am / Leave a Comment

Hey UDL schools! As your head coaches have notified you, the Yale Debate Association (the UDL’s sister organization at Yale) is hosting its annual Osterweis Debate Tournament next Sunday. You are encouraged to sign up! Please let us know if you have any questions. Here is the official invite.

————————————————————–

To the Debate Community of Connecticut,

The Yale Debate Association is pleased to invite you to the 33rd Annual Rollin G. Osterweis Debate Tournament on Sunday, April 7, 2013. This year’s theme is “Insight through Inquiry.” For those of you unfamiliar with the tournament, Osterweis is a free tournament for Connecticut high school students to experience the collegiate style of parliamentary debate. Participants will learn about parliamentary procedure in the morning with a demonstration round, and then debate with a partner for three preliminary rounds, before breaking to Semifinals and a Final Round to decide this year’s champion. Debates will focus on issues familiar to the average student, including American and international politics, ethics, and justice. With that said, given the level of competition at Osterweis in recent years, we highly recommend that students selected to attend have some prior experience with debate. Novices are welcome, but many find the experience most fulfilling having previously attended at least a couple of tournaments. Students will be given a free lunch and breakfast, and Yale debaters will judge and offer feedback. Coaches will have the opportunity to participate in coaching workshops during the day if they wish. Awards will be given to top varsity and teams and speakers.

Please register teams by filling out this form. We intend to cap the size of our tournament at 50 teams this year, and will therefore set a soft cap of two teams accepted per school before April 1st. We encourage you to register up to as many as five teams, because should we not reach our cap, we will be admitting additional teams, with final notification sent out after 11:59 PM on April 4th. We will try to accommodate any changes made to registration prior to this deadline, but ask that you notify us as soon as you can.

For more information on the schedule as well as other information, feel free to visit our website (which will be updated soon) or email us at osterweis@gmail.com. Feel free to pass this information on to other schools who might be interested.

We look forward to seeing you in April!

Best,
Nathaniel Rubin, Tournament Director (nathaniel.rubin@yale.edu)
Joanna Zheng, Tournament Director (joanna.zheng@yale.edu)
Ben Della Rocca, Assistant Tournament Director (benjamin.dellarocca@yale.edu)
Adira Levine, Assistant Tournament Director (adira.levine@yale.edu)

Tournament on Friday, March 29th

March 25, 2013 4:30 am / Leave a Comment

After several unfortunate weather-induced cancellations, the Urban Debate League is very excited to announce its next tournament this Friday!

Date: March 29th 2013 (Friday)
Time: 2:30 pm – 6 pm
Location: Yale University Campus

Logistics: Notify the head coach of each school the number of teams and names of students that will be attending the tournament about a week in advance. Depending on the location of the school, the head coach will arrange to meet up either at the high school or directly at Yale, usually at the New Haven Green or William Harkness Hall (100 Wall Street). There will be three rounds of parliamentary debate, each lasting roughly an hour. The topic and sides for each team will be announced before each round, and respective coaches will usually aid with preparations. Feedback will be given after each round. Dinner (Domino’s pizza!) will be served when the tournament ends. Top three speakers and teams will receive medals and gift certificates to Ashley’s Ice Cream. Ballots with comments will be returned to students via their coaches at their next UDL session.

Why You Should Come:
- No debating experience required. Students of all debating backgrounds welcome!
- Good judging and feedback. Coaches will help you prep.
- Great practice for UDL tournaments later in the year and the Osterweis Tournament.
- Free pizza and drinks. Winners get Ashley’s gift cards!
- Connect with students from other UDL schools

If you have any questions, please direct them to our Tournament Director Erin Cofrancesco at erin.cofrancesco (at) yale.edu. We look forward to seeing you at Yale this Friday.
